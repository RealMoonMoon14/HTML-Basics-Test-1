Starting with "wifi is ok if you're close to the router", this just holds a special meaning to me because it is a long running joke in the community I play with. In fighting games, a wired, stable connection is a neccessity. So "wifi is okay" is funny because wifi tends to be the most unstable, unviable connection type for what I play. "It is important to know why you are throwing them (fireballs) in the first place. Obviously they cause damage. But more importantly, they cause your opponent to dance." is something that also relates to fighting games, (can you tell that they are a big part of my life?). It embodies my playstyle and idea of leadership in life. I would rather be in the driver's seat, making the decisions and leading the way. Not being in control worries me a lot, so making my "opponent" or whoever I'm with dance to my beat is important. Lastly, "Isn't that just normal though?" links with the fact that I really struggle making connections. Fact of the matter is that I don't have many friends, and I struggle talking to people. I'm out of touch with most social norms. I don't use social media like Snapchat or Instagram. I feel empty whenever I walk by people in the halls. Talking to their friends, chatting it up. As I said before, being out of touch with what's going on today is a real kicker. I don't get most social cues, nor do I understand a lot of people's feelings or motivations if they do speak to me. But in the end, I am who I am. And that's my own normal.
